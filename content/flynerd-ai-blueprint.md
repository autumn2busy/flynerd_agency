## Step 1: Audit Your Current State

Before you automate anything, understand what you're working with.

**Questions to answer:**
- Where is your team spending the most time on repetitive tasks?
- What data do you collect, and where does it live?
- Which tools are you already using?
- What processes break most often?

**Quick exercise:** Track your team's activities for one week. Categorize tasks as "creative/strategic" vs. "repetitive/administrative." The repetitive tasks are your automation candidates.

---

## Step 2: Identify High-ROI Opportunities

Not all automation is worth the investment. Focus on areas with the highest return.

**Top 5 automation opportunities for most SMBs:**

1. **Customer inquiry handling** — AI chatbots for FAQ and qualification
2. **Email marketing** — Automated sequences based on behavior
3. **Data entry and syncing** — Connect your tools, eliminate manual input
4. **Reporting** — Auto-generated dashboards and summaries
5. **Content creation** — AI-assisted drafts and repurposing

**Rule of thumb:** Start with tasks that are high-volume, low-complexity, and well-documented.

## Step 4: Implement in Phases

Don't try to automate everything at once.

**Phase 1 (Week 1-2):** One quick win
- Pick your highest-impact, lowest-complexity automation
- Implement, test, and validate

**Phase 2 (Week 3-6):** Core workflows
- Build out 3-5 key automations
- Connect your primary tools
- Train your team

**Phase 3 (Ongoing):** Optimization
- Monitor performance
- Identify new opportunities
- Iterate and improve

---

## Step 5: Measure What Matters

Automation is only valuable if it delivers results.

**Metrics to track:**

- **Time saved:** Hours per week reclaimed from manual tasks
- **Response time:** How quickly you engage leads and customers
- **Conversion rates:** Are automated sequences performing?
- **Error reduction:** Fewer mistakes in data and processes
- **Revenue impact:** Direct and indirect revenue attributable to automation

**Pro tip:** Establish baselines before implementing automation so you can measure the delta.
